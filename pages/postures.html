Postures

In Thinking Blind, a "posture" is the way your heart and mind stand toward truth. It's less about what you know and more about how you're willing to wrestle with what's real.

Core Postures

  • Curious - willling to ask uncomftable questions.
  • Honest - refusing to lie to yourself just to feel safe.
  • Patient - lettng understanding unfold instead of forcing it.
  • Humble - open to being wrong without collapsing.

How to Practice These Postures

  1. Notice when you feel defensive or triggered by an idea
  2. Pause long enough to ask, "What truth might I be avoiding here?"
  3. Write down what you honestly think,, even if it's messy.
  4. Compare that with what's actually true over time, not just in the moment.
